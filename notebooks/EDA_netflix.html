Netflix catalogue analysis

Author: Pietro Tegon

Introduction

In this project, I aim to analyze the characteristics and patterns within Netflix's extensive catalog of movies and TV series to uncover key trends and insights. By examining various metrics such as ratings, hours viewed, genres, networks, and release years, I would like to understand the factors contributing to content success on the platform. Through visualizations and data-driven insights, I will explore the relationship between popularity and quality, the diversity of genres, and the impact of runtime and episode counts on ratings. These findings offer actionable recommendations for Netflix and content creators to optimize their offerings and cater to diverse audience preferences in a competitive streaming market.

Data sources

  1. netflix_titles.csv: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/rahulvyasm/netflix-movies-and-tv-shows

  2. total_netflix_2023 new.csv: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/willianoliveiragibin/netlifx-hour-2023/data

  3. TV_show_data (2).csv : https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/oleksiimartusiuk/1500-tv-shows-ranked

# Function to safely convert string representations of lists into actual lists
def convert_to_list(x):
    if isinstance(x, str):
        try:
            # Attempt to evaluate the string as a literal Python object (e.g., list, tuple, etc.)
            return ast.literal_eval(x) if x.startswith('[') and x.endswith(']') else x.split(', ')
        except:
            # If it's not a valid list-like string, just split it by commas
            return x.split(', ')
    return x

# Apply the convert_to_list function to the 'Genre' column

Conclusion

The findings of this project provide valuable insights into the dynamics of Netflix's movie and TV series offerings:

Movies:

There is a clear disconnect between the most-viewed movies and the highest-rated ones, indicating that popularity does not necessarily equate to critical acclaim. Highly-viewed movies often appeal to broad audiences but may receive mixed reviews due to diverse preferences. The near-normal distribution of movie ratings, clustered around 6.4, suggests Netflix's portfolio predominantly includes average-to-good content. However, there is room to identify and replicate the factors behind highly-rated movies to enhance the platforms appeal. The surge in movie releases after 2000 highlights Netflix's growth as a streaming giant, with its strategy focusing on scaling the catalog.

TV Series:

TV series ratings show a strong central tendency, with most series rated between 7 and 8. This reflects above-average quality but highlights the challenge of producing universally acclaimed content. Networks like JKN and STARZ outperform others in average ratings, likely due to niche, high-quality productions, while Netflix's diverse catalog results in a broader range of quality and ratings. Drama dominates as the most common genre, followed by comedy, action, and crime. Underrepresented genres, such as horror and thriller, present opportunities for Netflix to diversify its content and target niche audiences.

Shared Insights Across Movies and Series:

Viewer engagement, as measured by hours viewed, is influenced by subjective preferences rather than quantifiable factors like runtime or episode count, emphasizing the importance of personalized recommendations. High-quality content tends to be concise, as seen in shorter, highly-rated series compared to longer ones that often lose viewer interest over time. This finding suggests that focusing on well-paced, shorter storytelling could lead to higher ratings and viewer satisfaction.

Implications for Netflix:

Netflix could invest in high-quality productions in underrepresented genres or niche categories to attract untapped audiences. By analyzing the attributes of critically successful and highly-viewed content, Netflix can refine its strategy for producing and promoting new releases. Personalized recommendations based on user behavior and preferences could significantly enhance engagement and satisfaction, bridging the gap between popularity and quality. In conclusion, this project highlights the importance of balancing mass appeal with critical acclaim and maintaining a diverse yet high-quality catalog. As the streaming landscape grows increasingly competitive, leveraging these insights could position Netflix to sustain its leadership in the industry while delivering exceptional content that resonates with audiences worldwide.
